Lesson 01 — The Shrinking Yardstick

"Millionaire" Doesn't Mean
What It Used To

In 1999, roughly 1 in every 1,200 Americans was a millionaire — hitting seven figures was national-television material. Today it's about 1 in 150, and UBS projects more than 3,000 new millionaires minted every day for the rest of the decade. Not because people are creating more value — because the yardstick itself is shrinking.

MEASURING IN DOLLARS

  • Supply expands every year — every new dollar debases the ones you hold
  • Millionaire status is being minted by inflation, not achievement
  • Your purchasing power quietly leaks while the number stays the same
  • You can work harder and still fall behind — the denominator is broken

MEASURING IN SATS

  • 21 million BTC — 2.1 quadrillion sats — forever. Cannot be debased
  • 1,000,000 sats = 0.01 BTC: a yardstick that never shrinks
  • Adding value to society earns you more of a fixed pie, not a diluted one
  • Proof of work — your measure of wealth is finally honest
The mindset shift this course asks of you: stop measuring your wealth in a currency that's being printed, and start measuring it in the only asset that can't be. The new yardstick is one million sats.
Lesson 02 — The Scarcity Math

There Isn't Enough
To Go Around

Run the numbers on 21 million coins against 8 billion people and the conclusion is unavoidable: most of the world can never own a meaningful slice. The people who understand this first win.

21,000,000
Total Bitcoin, ever. An estimated 4–6 million are already lost forever — leaving roughly 15–17 million actually in circulation.
2,100,000,000
Maximum possible 0.01 BTC positions on Earth. Even with perfect distribution, only ~25% of humanity could ever hold one — and whales, ETFs, and institutions already hold massive chunks.
~200,000 sats
Each person's "fair share." Divide all sats by 8 billion people, and roughly only 1 in 3–4 people can ever be a sat-millionaire.
~120,000
Wallets holding $1M+ of Bitcoin — versus 62 million dollar-millionaires. Being a Bitcoin millionaire is roughly 500× rarer, and multi-wallet holders only strengthen the point.
~48,000,000
Cold-storage wallets holding even $1 of BTC. In a world of 8 billion people, simply getting off zero — in self-custody — is a monumental head start.
You don't need a whole coin to win. 0.01 BTC — one million sats — already puts you statistically ahead of billions of people. The window for that milestone is what's closing.

Lesson 03 — Provably Finite

The Only Asset You
Can't Make More Of

More dollars get printed. More houses get built. More gold gets mined — the ocean floor hasn't even been touched. More stock gets issued. Bitcoin is the first asset in history that is provably, verifiably finite — and its issuance schedule is locked to the year 2140.

10 min

A new block, on average — governed by an automatic difficulty adjustment that needs no human, no CEO, no headquarters.

÷2 every 4 yrs

The halving. Each cycle, new issuance is cut in half — by 2032, only dust remains to be mined.

~450 BTC/day

Current daily issuance this epoch. Public companies alone are buying nearly 4× that — every single day.

10 min audit

Auditing Fort Knox takes 18–24 months. Auditing Bitcoin's entire supply takes one block. Verifiable scarcity, on demand.

01

SELF-CUSTODY = REAL OWNERSHIP

Your stocks sit at a broker. Your cash sits at a bank. Your "ownership" is a promise someone else keeps. Self-custodied Bitcoin is possession itself — it can't be frozen, seized, or shut off, and you can cross any border carrying billions in your head. Capital controls stop $10,000 in cash at the airport; they can't stop twelve words.

02

TRUST NO ONE — VERIFY EVERYTHING

Every legacy system runs on trust: trust the bank, trust the custodian, trust the pilot with your gold. Trust is only required where fraud is possible. Bitcoin removes the requirement — the ledger is open, the math is public, and no one can fake their value on the blockchain.

03

PROOF OF WORK, NOT PROOF OF STAKE

In proof-of-stake systems, the biggest holders rule the network — success by inheritance. In proof of work, security is earned with real energy, the way rewards should be. Attacked by state-level hackers every minute for 15+ years, and every 10 minutes the chain grows exponentially harder to break. Never hacked. Never will be.
